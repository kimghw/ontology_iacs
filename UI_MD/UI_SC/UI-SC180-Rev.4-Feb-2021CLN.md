# SC 180

## Hold, ballast and dry space water level detectors and Performance Standards for Water Level Detectors on Bulk Carriers and Single Hold Cargo Ships other than Bulk Carriers (Resolution MSC.188(79))

**(Sep 2005)**
**(Rev.1 May 2004)**
**(Rev.2 Nov 2005)**
**(Rev.3 Mar 2012)**
**(Rev.4 Feb 2021)**

Interpretation of Regulation 25 of SOLAS Chapter II-1 as amended by IMO resolutions up to MSC.436(99) and Regulation 12 of SOLAS Chapter XII as amended by IMO resolutions up to MSC.170(79)

### Introduction

When water level detectors are installed on single hold cargo ships other than bulk carriers subject to Regulation 25 of SOLAS Chapter II-1 or bulk carriers subject to Regulation 12 of SOLAS Chapter XII, the Performance Standards for water level detectors on bulk carriers and single hold cargo ships other than bulk carriers, annexed to IMO Resolution MSC.188(79) adopted on 3 December 2004 are to be applied.

**Note:**

1.  IACS Members are to implement this UI and its referenced standards for equipment approval requests received on or after 1 October 2003.
2.  Rev.1 is to be uniformly implemented by IACS Members from 1 July 2004.
3.  Rev.2 is to be uniformly implemented by IACS Members on ships contracted for construction on or after 1 January 2007.
4.  The "contracted for construction" date means the date on which the contract to build the vessel is signed between the prospective owner and the shipbuilder.
5.  Rev.3 reflects that MSC.188(79) superseded and revoked MSC.145(77). Rev.3 is to be uniformly implemented by IACS Members on ships contracted for construction on or after 1 July 2012.
6.  Rev.4 is to be uniformly implemented by IACS Members on ships contracted for construction on or after 1 July 2022.

## Interpretations

**Performance Standards, paragraph 3.2.1** - Detector system should provide a reliable indication of water reaching a preset level.

**Interpretation:** One sensor capable of detecting both preset levels (pre-alarm level and main alarm level) is allowed.

---

**Performance Standards, paragraph 3.2.3** - Detection equipment should be suitably corrosion resistant for all intended cargoes.

**Interpretation:** Detection equipment includes the sensor and any filter and protection arrangements for the detector installed in cargo holds and other spaces as required by SOLAS regulation II-1/25 or XII/12.1.

---

**Performance Standards, paragraph 3.2.5** - The part of the system which has circuitry in the cargo area, should be intrinsically safe.

**Interpretation:**
*   In general, the construction and type testing is to be in accordance with IEC 60079-11:2011 to a minimum requirement of EX(ib).
*   The maximum surface temperature of equipment installed within cargo spaces is not to exceed 85 deg. C where characteristics of dust and gases are unknown.
*   Where intrinsically safe equipment is installed, it is to be of a certified safe type.

---

**Performance Standards, paragraph 3.3.2** - Visual and audible alarms should conform to the Code on Alarms and Indicators.

**Interpretation:** The pre-alarm, as a primary alarm, is to indicate a condition that requires prompt attention. The main-alarm, as an emergency alarm, is to indicate that immediate actions are to be taken.

---

**Performance Standards, paragraph 3.3.5** - Override capability for tanks designed for water ballast.

**Interpretation:** The water ingress alarm system is not to be capable of overriding the alarm of spaces not designed nor intended to carry water ballast. Enabling override is to be customized for each specific ship prior to commissioning tests.

---

**Performance Standards, paragraph 3.3.7** - Continuous monitoring and fault detection.

**Interpretation:** Fault monitoring is to address faults associated with the system, e.g. open circuit, short circuit, loss of power supplies, CPU failure.

---

**Performance Standards, paragraph 3.3.8** - Electrical power from two independent supplies.

**Interpretation:**
*   The electrical power supply is to be from two separate sources, one from the main source and one from the emergency source, unless a continuously charged dedicated accumulator battery is fitted.
*   Where batteries are used for secondary power supply, failure alarms for both power supplies are to be provided.

---

**Performance Standards, paragraph 3.4.1** - Testing references.

**Interpretation:**
*   IACS UR E10 may be used as an equivalent test standard to IEC 60092-504:2016.
*   Range of tests includes functional tests, power supply tests, environmental tests, vibration tests, EMC tests, insulation and high voltage tests.

---

**Performance Standards, APPENDIX, paragraph 2.1.1.1** - IP68 enclosure requirements.

**Interpretation:**
*   Submerged test period for ballast tanks: not less than 20 days.
*   Submerged test period for dry spaces: not less than 24 hours.

---

**Performance Standards, APPENDIX, paragraph 2.1.1.2** - Cargo/water mixture testing.

**Interpretation:** Type testing with four cargo groups: iron ore, coal, grain, and aggregate (sand) particles in seawater at 50% concentration by weight, with ten consecutive tests without cleaning filter arrangements.

---

**Performance Standards, APPENDIX, paragraph 2.2.1** - Sensor location.

**Interpretation:** If sensors are not placed within 1 corrugation space or 1 bulkhead vertical stiffener space from the centreline, sensors are to be located at both the port and starboard sides of the cargo hold.

---

**Performance Standards, APPENDIX, Section 4** - Manuals.

**Interpretation:** For each ship, a copy of the manual is to be made available to the surveyor at least 24 hours prior to survey.
