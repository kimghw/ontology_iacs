# LL29 Sheer Credit for Superimposed Superstructures (Regulation 38(5), 38(7) and 38(12))

**(1972)**
**(Rev.1 1983)**
**(Rev.2 July 2008)**

---

**(a) Regulation 38(5): Superstructures superimposed on a complete superstructure.**

In applying Regulation 38(5) (sheer on a complete superstructure ship) where there is an enclosed poop or forecastle superimposed on a complete superstructure, sheer credit shall be allowed for such a poop or forecastle, according to the method of Regulation 38(12) as shown in Fig 1.

**Fig. 1**

[Image: A diagram illustrating sheer credit for a superstructure superimposed on a complete superstructure. The horizontal axis represents the length of the ship, with 'F.T.' (Forward Terminal) at the right end. The vertical axis represents height. Two primary horizontal lines are shown: the lower 'Freeboard deck' and the upper 'Superstructure deck'. Between these decks, 'Standard height' is indicated. A parabolic sheer curve, labeled '1', starts from a point 'X' on the superstructure deck and extends towards the bow. A second, higher parabolic curve, labeled '2', represents the virtual sheer line for the superimposed forecastle. Various ordinates are marked: 'l' is the length of the superimposed forecastle, 'h' is its height above the superstructure deck. 'Z' is the sheer credit ordinate at the end of the ship relative to the superstructure deck. 'Zv' is the virtual sheer credit ordinate at the end of the ship relative to the virtual sheer line.]

**(b) Regulation 38(7): Superstructures superimposed on a forecastle or poop (i.e. a stepped forecastle or poop).**

In applying Regulation 38(7) and 38(12) where a poop or forecastle consists of two layers, the method shown in Fig 2 shall be used:

**Fig. 2**

[Image: A diagram showing sheer credit for a stepped forecastle or poop with two layers. The horizontal axis has 'F.T.' at the right end. The vertical axis shows height. A 'Freeboard deck' is the baseline. Above it, a stepped superstructure is shown. The first tier has a length 'l1' and the second, superimposed tier has a length 'l2' and height 'h'. 'Standard height' is indicated from the freeboard deck. Two sheer curves are shown: curve '1' corresponds to the first tier, and curve '2' corresponds to the second, superimposed tier. 'Z' is the sheer credit ordinate for the first tier. 'Zv' is the virtual sheer credit ordinate, calculated as `Zv = Z * l1 / l2`, applicable to the second tier.]

---
**LL29 (cont)**

In the above the following definitions apply:

Z is as per Regulation 38(5).

Zv is the end ordinate of a virtual standard parabolic curve taken through the point "X". If Zv is greater than (Z+h), the end ordinate shall be (Z+h), in which case point "X" shall be disregarded and curve (2) not taken into account.

When the length of the first tier superstructure is greater than 0.5L, the virtual standard parabolic curve shall commence at amidships as indicated in Fig 1.

---
**Footnote:** This UI is applicable to Regulations 38(5), 38(7) and 38(12) of the 1988 Protocol.
