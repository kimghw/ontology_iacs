# LL17 Minimum bow height (Regulation 39(1) and 39(2))

**(1968)**
**(Corr. 1996)**
**(Corr.1 June 2006)**
**(Corr.2 Oct 2007)**
**(Rev.1 July 2008)**

When a ship built on or after 21 July 1968 is arranged to suit exceptional operational requirements such that the forecastle and/or sheer forward do not meet the provisions of paragraphs (1) and (2) of Regulation 39 of the 1966 ICLL, the increase of calculated summer freeboard may, with the concurrence of the Administration, be determined in the following ways.

## Nomenclature

*   **ΔE₀ ≥ 0**: Bow height penalty
*   **Smin = Hmin - fbds**: Minimum bow height calculated according to paragraph (1) of this Regulation
*   **fbds**: Summer freeboard
*   **d ≤ 0.15L**: Extent of sheer (measured from FP)
*   **lF ≤ 0.07L**: Mean covered length of forecastle
*   **h**: Height of forecastle measured at FP from zero sheer line
*   **SFP**: Actual sheer measured at FP
*   **SBHD**: Actual sheer in way of forecastle bulkhead
*   **S₁ = Smin (0.15L - lF / 0.15L)²**: Theoretical sheer in way of forecastle bulkhead corresponding to Smin
*   **S₂ = h (0.15L - lF / 0.15L)²**: Theoretical sheer in way of forecastle bulkhead corresponding to h

**Note:** This UI may also be applied to Regulation 39(1) and 39(2) of the 1988 Protocol and the revised 1988 Protocol upon the special consideration by the Administration.

---

# LL17 (cont)

**a)** Where no forecastle is fitted and the sheer forward extends less than 0.15L from fore perpendicular (FP):

ΔE₀ = Smin - SFP (d / 0.15L) ≥ 0
0 ≤ SFP ≤ 1.5Smin

Key points and dimensions are labeled: "FP" (Fore Perpendicular), "SFP" (Actual sheer at FP), "Smin" (Minimum bow height), "Hmin" (Minimum bow height), "fbds" (Summer freeboard), and "d" (Extent of sheer). **b)** Where there is no sheer on the forward part of the freeboard deck, and the forecastle length is less than 0.07L from FP:

ΔE₀ = Smin - h (F / 0.07L) ≥ 0
0 ≤ h ≤ 1.5Smin

Labeled dimensions include "FP," "hBHD" (Actual sheer in way of forecastle bulkhead), "F," "h" (Height of forecastle), "Smin" (Minimum bow height), and "Hmin" (Minimum bow height). The height of the forecastle at the bulkhead position shall not be less than the ordinate, at that point, of a parabolic sheer curve having an ordinate Smin at the forward perpendicular and extending aft for a distance of 0.15L from the forward perpendicular.

---

# LL17 (cont)

**c)** Where the sheer forward extends less than 0.15L and the length of forecastle is less than 0.07L from FP:

**(i)** Smin ≤ h ≤ 1.5Smin
0 ≤ SBHD ≤ 1.5S₁

ΔE₀ = Smin (1 - h / Smin (lF / 0.07L)) (1 - SBHD / S₁ (d - lF / 0.15L - lF)) ≥ 0

**Image:** A diagram of a ship's forward profile with a "Forecastle deck," "Effective deck line," "Actual deck line," and "Parabolic sheer line." Labels include "FP" (Fore Perpendicular), "lF" (Mean covered length of forecastle), "h" (Height of forecastle), "hBHD" (Actual sheer in way of forecastle bulkhead), "Smin" (Minimum bow height), "SBHD" (Actual sheer in way of forecastle bulkhead), "Hmin" (Minimum bow height), "S₁", "d" (Extent of sheer), "0.15L", and "0.07L".

**Conditions:**

(1 - h / Smin (lF / 0.07L))
(1 - SBHD / S₁ (d - lF / 0.15L - lF))

not to be taken negative (less than zero)

The height of the forecastle at bulkhead must satisfy the same conditions as in subparagraph (b) of this paragraph.

---

# LL17 (cont)

**(ii)** h ≤ Smin
0 ≤ SBHD ≤ 1.5S₂

ΔE₀ = (Smin - h) + (1 - lF / 0.07L) (1 - SBHD / S₂ (d - lF / 0.15L - lF)) ≥ 0

**Image:** A ship's forward profile diagram showing a "Forecastle deck," "Effective deck line," "Actual deck line," and "Parabolic sheer line." Labeled components are "FP" (Fore Perpendicular), "Smin" (Minimum bow height), "Hmin" (Minimum bow height), "h" (Height of forecastle), "hBHD" (Actual sheer in way of forecastle bulkhead), "SBHD" (Actual sheer in way of forecastle bulkhead), "S₂", "0.07L", "d" (Extent of sheer), and "0.15L".

**Conditions:**

(1 - lF / 0.07L)
(1 - SBHD / S₂ (d - lF / 0.15L - lF))

not to be taken negative (less than zero)

The height of the forecastle at the bulkhead position shall not be less than the ordinate, at that point, or a parabolic sheer curve having an ordinate h at the forward perpendicular and extending aft for a distance of 0.15L from the forward perpendicular.

In general, this interpretation should be applied to existing ships only. However, to suit exceptional operational requirements, and upon the special consideration by the Administration, the provision of this interpretation may also be applied to new ships.
